How to Use Bunny Budget

1. Setup Your Income & Bills

Go to the Manage tab. First, enter your monthly **Salary**. Then, add all your recurring costs (like 'Rent' or 'Internet') as a **Bill** category.

2. Add Your Categories

In Manage, add your flexible spending categories (like 'Groceries' or 'Gas') as an **Envelope**. Add your goals (like 'Vacation' or 'Credit Card') as a **Saving** or **Debt** category.

3. Assign Your Money

Go to the Manage tab. Add any **Other Income** you received. The app shows your "Money to Assign". Assign this money into your Bills, Envelopes, Savings, and Debts until it reaches zero.

4. Track Your Spending

On the Dashboard tab, press the big **pink '+' button** to add an expense. Select the **Envelope** you spent from (e.g., "Groceries").

5. Fix Mistakes

Made a typo? On the Dashboard, click an **Envelope** card. A list of all its transactions will appear. Click a transaction to **Edit** or **Delete** it.

Budgeting Terms Explained

Salary

Your total *recurring* monthly income. This is the baseline for your budget.

Other Income

One-time or variable income (like a side job or selling an item). Add this on the Manage page.

Category: Bill

For recurring costs, like Rent, Utilities, or a Car Payment. You assign money to these first to make sure they're paid.

Category: Envelope

Your "digital envelope" for flexible spending, like Groceries, Gas, or Dining Out. You spend from these using the '+' button.

Category: Saving

Your "digital savings jar" for a positive goal, like Vacation or an Emergency Fund. Money you assign here builds up over time.

Category: Debt

A "digital envelope" to pay off a specific debt, like a Credit Card. This is separate from a fixed 'Bill' (like a car payment) and is for extra paydown.
